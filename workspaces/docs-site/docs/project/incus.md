<h1 id="meet-incus-title">Meet Incus.</h1>

<p class="inc-incus-intro__lead">Incus is the armored alpaca–llama who turns up around Incan when something deserves a closer look—or when a successful build deserves a quiet celebration.</p>

He is not the product, the documentation narrator, or the hero of every screen. He is an Easter egg: a little warmth and personality tucked around a language and toolchain that still take their work seriously.

<section class="inc-incus-section" markdown="1">

## Incapunk, in character

Incus belongs to the **Incapunk** visual language: an Incan-inspired, futuristic style built from dark graphite surfaces, forged gold structure, and controlled cyan light. The character keeps that same balance—distinctive without becoming noisy.

<div class="inc-incus-profile">
  <article><span>Form</span><strong>Stylized alpaca–llama</strong><p>A long, readable silhouette with dark tan fur and no white mane.</p></article>
  <article><span>Armor</span><strong>Gold over a dark underlayer</strong><p>Metal should read as metal, with cyan seams and a circular illuminated core.</p></article>
  <article><span>Temperament</span><strong>Calm, observant, capable</strong><p>Curious and occasionally mischievous, but never frantic or cartoonish.</p></article>
  <article><span>Habitat</span><strong>The edges of the system</strong><p>First runs, hints, warnings, completed tasks, and corners worth inspecting.</p></article>
</div>

</section>

## If you spot him

Incus appears when the documentation changes mode: a useful hint, a Python or Rust mental-model bridge, a composed failure, or a small moment of success. Different poses may appear over time, but the context should always make sense.

The character stays constant. Meaning comes from what he is doing.

<section class="inc-incus-rule" markdown="1">

## The rule

> If Incus is the first thing you notice, we probably used him too loudly.

The page should still work without him. Incus rewards attention; he does not demand it.
